*The basic strapline is: An AI assistant for disabled ex-software engineer. Built as a tool. As a learning exercise. And as a distraction from the pain, fatigue, and the fact I can't code professionally any more. It keeps my claws sharp.*

## Introduction

I have rather mixed opinions regarding Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning and Large Language Models. Part of me is incredibly excited and optimistic about the possibilities, but then another part of me is absolutely terrified of what could go wrong. Its a tightrope to walk while trying to learn enough about it to see the full picture.

I've worked in technology, the web and software development since the early 90's so i think i can say I'm a bit of a dinosaur. I remember the gold rush. The dot com boom. And the wild frontier. Nearly 30yrs at the code-face, good and bad.

Unsurprisingly, I've been doing my best to keep up with the developments in AI. From the "nah, its all hype" 4-5 yrs ago, to being absolutely blown away by some of the things AI, LLMS, and most recently Agents are capable of.

When Ollama was released, I started playing with local LLMs, and then, got sucked into the subscription model with a cloud provider, paying ££ a month. At this point, may i recommend [Abacus](https://abacus.ai)? Pay one monthly fee, get access to all the big models, including the image generators. \*

Then, i decided, to believe the hype and build my own JARVIS, using "vibe coding" like the YouTubers are yelling about.

**Take it from me, thats bollocks. A good agent takes quite a bit of work.**

So, here we are. This little adventure starts about 2 weeks in to my 5th attempt. Each attempt had sanity and reason, right up until they didnt. Each time hours were spent. Sometimes doing nothing more than screaming obscenities at my laptop.

This time though, so far seems a little more hopeful. This time I'm not trying to build the thing from scratch. This time, I'm going to build on the shoulder of giants. This time, like [OpenClaw](https://openclaw.ai), I'm building on Pi. And the results have been remarkable (so far).

## But enough history. What, exactly is J.A.N.E?

J.A.N.E is literally, Just Another Nerdy Experiment. An exploration of what i can build at home, on my own kit. (I'll post tech gubbins in a later post), both in terms of cost, and in terms of capability. With a caveat: I AM using some of the larger cloud models for some of the building processes of the harness extensions, and again, I'll post about that at some point too.

And this was the first post in a series, where i share my experience, lessons learnt etc. With a twist.

I'm going to let J.A.N.E add responses to the things I say. This for me is the interesting target. The vision for the assistant is to learn about me over time, based on our interactions (well, thats the aim anyway, watch this space!). So, in my mind at least, over time, it should be able to respond and offer some interesting insights, as the capability grows.

Buckle up, its going to be quite a ride.

In my next post, if all goes to plan, I'll ask J.A.N.E add "her" thoughts. If not, expect me to be moaning about things you might not care about
